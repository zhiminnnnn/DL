## ðŸ§ Course Reflection
>The study of Digital Logic has proven to be highly informative in the quest to understand the foundation and application of digital systems. Initially, concepts such as number systems, logic gates, and Boolean algebra were perceived as complex and difficult to grasp thoroughly. However, after the delivery of several lectures and tutorials, significant progress in the comprehension of these basic principles was achieved.
>
>The reinforcement of learning was actively supported by lab sessions, which were considered significant to the overall understanding of the subject. Theoretical knowledge was integrated into practical application through the use of Deeds Software, where digital circuits were designed and simulated.
>
>Through circuit design tasks involving both combinational and sequential circuits, the analysis of circuit performance was taught. This allowed for faults to be traced and designs to be enhanced by understanding the limitations of system performance. Furthermore, learning was developed through project-based work, where the importance of teamwork, clear communication, and task coordination was emphasized to ensure that project objectives were met efficiently.
